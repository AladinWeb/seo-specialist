How Long Does Conveyancing Usually Take in Australia?

One of the most common questions when buying or selling property is: "How long will conveyancing take?" In 2025, the answer varies, but with digital advancements and efficient practices, timelines are shorter than ever. This guide breaks down typical durations, factors that influence speed, and tips to ensure a smooth, timely settlement — including why partnering with experts like First Class Legal can make all the difference.

Standard Conveyancing Timeline in Australia (2025)

The conveyancing process generally runs from contract exchange to settlement. Here's the average breakdown:

  • Contract Review & Exchange: 1–7 days
  • Searches & Enquiries: 2–4 weeks
  • Finance Approval (for buyers): 2–3 weeks (if not pre-approved)
  • Final Preparations & Adjustments: 1–2 weeks
  • Settlement Day: Electronic via PEXA (instant funds transfer)

Average Total: 6–8 weeks for straightforward transactions. Complex cases (e.g., off-the-plan or subdivisions) can take 10–12 weeks or longer.

State-by-State Variations

  • Victoria & Queensland: Often 30–90 days contract periods; electronic settlements standard.
  • NSW: 42 days common; PEXA mandatory for most.
  • WA & SA: Flexible, often 4–8 weeks.

In 2025, nationwide adoption of digital platforms like PEXA has standardized and accelerated processes across states.

Factors That Can Speed Up or Delay Conveyancing

Speed Boosters

  • Pre-approval finance → Shaves weeks off
  • Electronic contracts & signatures → Instant exchange
  • Proactive conveyancer → Fast searches & follow-ups
  • No chain (e.g., vacant land) → Often under 6 weeks
  • PEXA settlements → Funds transfer same day

Common Delays

  • Finance delays (buyer not pre-approved)
  • Council or strata searches taking longer
  • Off-the-plan or new titles
  • Property chain issues (waiting on other sales)
  • Unexpected issues (e.g., encumbrances, disputes)
