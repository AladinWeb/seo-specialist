How to Avoid Being Marked as Spam

To keep my sites safe, I focus on creating content that prioritizes user value, aligning with Google’s E-E-A-T principles (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness). I write in-depth articles—typically 1,500+ words—with actionable insights backed by credible sources. For instance, my recent post addressing reader pain points directly saw a 12% traffic increase.

Backlinks are another critical area. I avoid shady link-buying schemes and instead collaborate with reputable sites for guest posts. Working with industry influencers for authentic partnerships has boosted my sites’ authority without risking penalties. A friend who ignored this saw their site flagged for unnatural links, costing them weeks of cleanup.

Technical SEO is just as vital. I ensure my sites are mobile-optimized and load under three seconds—Google’s algorithms now factor in user experience heavily. Monthly audits using Google Search Console help me catch issues like broken links or thin pages early. Staying updated via webmaster forums and official announcements has saved me from pitfalls in past updates.
