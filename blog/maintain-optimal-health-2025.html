Sleep: The Foundation of Health

Sleep was my biggest health blind spot. I used to get five hours nightly, leaving me foggy and irritable. Now, I aim for 7-8 hours, as recommended by the National Sleep Foundation. A consistent bedtime—10 p.m. for me—improved my focus by 20%, per my own tracking.

Create a sleep-friendly environment. I use blackout curtains and a white noise machine, cutting wake-ups by half. A friend banned screens an hour before bed, saying, “My sleep tracker showed 30% better rest!” Blue light from devices disrupts melatonin, so I switch to reading books.

Avoid caffeine after 2 p.m. I made this change, and my sleep latency dropped from 30 to 10 minutes. Studies show caffeine can linger in your system for 8 hours, so timing matters. Herbal teas like chamomile are my go-to now.

If sleep issues persist, consult a doctor. My colleague discovered sleep apnea through a $200 test, and treatment boosted his energy by 40%. Good sleep isn’t a luxury—it’s a pillar of health.
