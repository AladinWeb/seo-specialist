Lakers Hold Off Pelicans 111-103: LeBron and Luka Drop 30 Each in Road Win

By John Paul Pajaro January 7, 2026
LAKERS VS PELICANS

The Los Angeles Lakers kicked off their road trip with a hard-fought 111-103 victory over the New Orleans Pelicans on January 6, extending the Pelicans' losing streak to eight games. In a matchup of contrasting fortunes — the contending Lakers (23-11) against the struggling Pelicans (8-30) — it was the superstar duo of LeBron James and Luka Dončić that made the difference, each pouring in 30 points to power LA to the win.

Star Power Prevails

LeBron and Luka combined for 60 points on efficient shooting, with James adding his usual playmaking flair and Dončić orchestrating the offense with precision. Despite missing key rotation players like Austin Reaves and Rui Hachimura due to calf injuries, the Lakers got timely contributions from Jake LaRavia and others off the bench. Deandre Ayton dominated the paint, helping LA control the boards and second-chance opportunities.

Pelicans' Struggles Continue

New Orleans fought hard but couldn't overcome their ongoing issues with turnovers and inconsistent scoring. Zion Williamson showed flashes of brilliance, but the team as a whole lacked the cohesion to mount a sustained comeback. Costly turnovers in crunch time allowed the Lakers to pull away in the fourth quarter, dominating the final period to secure the eight-point victory.

Key Takeaways

For the Lakers, this win reinforces their status as a top Western Conference contender — resilient even on the road and without full health. The Pelicans, meanwhile, face mounting questions as their season slips further away. With tough matchups ahead, New Orleans needs answers fast.

Personal opinion: Watching LeBron (still elite at 41) and Luka mesh like this is a treat. The Lakers look dangerous when these two are clicking — could this duo carry them deep into the playoffs?
