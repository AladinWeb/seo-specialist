How to Choose a Reliable Car Shipping Service

Choosing the right car shipping company is critical to avoid scams or delays. My first step is always checking credentials. Look for brokers or carriers with an A+ BBB rating and FMCSA licensing. I used a broker with a 4.8/5 rating on Transport Reviews, and their transparency gave me confidence. A friend who ignored this advice ended up with a fly-by-night company that delayed delivery by two weeks.

Compare quotes, but don’t chase the cheapest option. I got quotes ranging from $800 to $1,400 for my SUV. The lowest bid raised red flags—no reviews, vague terms—so I chose a mid-range option with clear insurance details. Always request a binding quote to avoid surprise fees, which happened to a colleague who paid $200 extra for “terminal handling.”

Read reviews on platforms like Trustpilot or Google. I found a carrier with 95% positive feedback, praising their communication. One review said, “Tracking updates kept me calm during my $1,000 move.” Also, verify insurance coverage—reputable companies offer at least $100,000 per vehicle. My carrier provided $150,000, ensuring peace of mind for my SUV.

Finally, clarify transport type: open carriers are cheaper (around $800-$1,200 for sedans), while enclosed ones (up to $2,000) protect high-value cars. I chose open transport to save $400, but for a vintage car, I’d go enclosed. Ask about delivery timelines and tracking—my service offered real-time GPS updates, which was a lifesaver.

Tips to Avoid Common Car Shipping Pitfalls

Avoiding scams starts with research. I always check a company’s FMCSA license and read at least 20 reviews to gauge reliability. A client who skipped this step lost $500 to a broker that vanished after payment. Stick to companies with at least a 4.5/5 rating and clear terms.

Understand the contract. I once overlooked a “terminal-to-terminal” clause, forcing me to pick up my car 50 miles from my destination. Always confirm door-to-door if that’s your preference. Also, inspect your vehicle on delivery—my carrier noted a small scratch, which their insurance covered ($200 repair) because I documented it immediately.

Plan ahead to avoid rush fees. I booked my shipment three weeks early, saving $150 compared to last-minute rates. Finally, communicate with your carrier. My driver’s daily updates kept me informed, preventing the anxiety a friend felt when her carrier went silent for days.
