The Most Anticipated Movies of 2026: Blockbusters That Will Define the Year

By John Paul Pajaro January 9, 2026
MOVIES 2026

2026 is already shaping up to be a monumental year for cinema. With visionary directors tackling epic stories, massive franchise returns, and bold new visions hitting the big screen, movie fans have plenty to get excited about. From Christopher Nolan's grand adaptation of a classic tale to the next chapters in Marvel and Dune universes, here’s my curated top 10 most anticipated films of the year — blending hype, director pedigree, star power, and personal excitement.

#1. The Odyssey (Christopher Nolan)

Nolan goes full epic with his adaptation of Homer’s timeless tale. Shot entirely on IMAX 70mm film, this star-studded spectacle promises breathtaking visuals and Nolan’s signature mind-bending storytelling. With a cast including Zendaya, Robert Pattinson, Tom Holland, and more, it’s poised to be the cinematic event of the year.

#2. Avengers: Doomsday (Russo Brothers)

The MCU rebuilds momentum with Robert Downey Jr.'s shocking return (as Doctor Doom?). Chris Evans and others rejoin the fray in what could be the biggest superhero gathering yet. After recent stumbles, this feels like Marvel's make-or-break moment — and the hype is already through the roof.

#3. Dune: Messiah (Denis Villeneuve)

Villeneuve completes his Dune trilogy with the adaptation of Frank Herbert’s complex sequel. Timothée Chalamet, Zendaya, and the incredible ensemble return for more desert power struggles and philosophical depth. If it matches the first two films' visual mastery, it's instant classic territory.

#4. Spider-Man: Brand New Day (Destin Daniel Cretton)

Tom Holland swings back for a fresh start post-No Way Home. With Zendaya and new faces like Sadie Sink, this street-level reboot promises grounded stakes and emotional depth. Marvel fans are starving for a great solo Spidey film — this could deliver.

#5. Toy Story 5 (Pixar)

Buzz, Woody, and the gang face "Toy meets Tech" in Pixar's latest. With returning voices and new emotional beats, it’s nostalgia done right. Pixar’s track record speaks for itself — expect tears and laughs in equal measure.

#6. The Super Mario Galaxy Movie

Mario goes cosmic in this animated sequel. Chris Pratt, Jack Black, and the crew explore gravity-defying worlds inspired by the beloved Wii classic. After the first film's massive success, this could be even bigger and more inventive.

#7. Wuthering Heights (Emerald Fennell)

Fennell (Saltburn, Promising Young Woman) brings sexy, chaotic energy to Emily Brontë’s classic. Margot Robbie and Jacob Elordi as the doomed lovers? This adaptation looks unhinged in the best way — dark romance reborn.

#8. Practical Magic 2

Nicole Kidman and Sandra Bullock return as the spellbinding Owens sisters. Midnight margaritas and sisterhood magic — pure comfort viewing with a supernatural twist. Nostalgia wave incoming.

#9. Project Hail Mary (Lord & Miller)

Ryan Gosling stars in this hard sci-fi adaptation of Andy Weir’s novel. Lone astronaut saving humanity with science and wit — from the producers of Spider-Verse, expect smart thrills and stunning visuals.

#10. Werwulf (Robert Eggers)

Eggers follows Nosferatu with medieval werewolf horror starring Lily-Rose Depp and Aaron Taylor-Johnson. Dark, atmospheric, and brutal — if you love The Witch or The Northman, this is essential.
